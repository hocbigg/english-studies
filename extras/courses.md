# **Alternatives & Resilience**

Below are reliable alternates for each core domain.

### **Close Reading / Intro Lit**

* *Backup:* Yale OCW “Introduction to Theory of Literature” (similar to MIT)
* *Textbook fallback:* *Abrams: A Glossary of Literary Terms* (authoritative), or free **Literary Studies Toolkit** by Duke University.

### **Writing / Composition**

* *Backup:* Georgia Tech edX “English Composition I”
* *Open text:* *Writing Spaces* (open, multi-volume).

### **Linguistics**

* *Backup:* MIT OCW “24.900 Intro to Linguistics” (multiple versions)
* *Open text:* *Essentials of Linguistics* (open-access).

### **Theory**

* *Backup:* Yale OCW “Introduction to Theory of Literature”
* *Textbook fallback:* *Rivkin & Ryan: Literary Theory: An Anthology* (excellent).

### **Research Methods**

* *Backup:* Coursera “Understanding Research” (UCI)

### **Catch-up Modules**

* **Writing:** OWL Purdue modules (free).
* **Grammar:** *U. Queensland English Grammar and Style* (self-paced).
* **Research:** Zotero tutorials + MLA Handbook.

# **Semester-by-Semester / Phase-by-Phase Roadmap (1–2 year plan)**

### **Phase 1 (Months 1–3) — Orientation**

* Reading Fiction (MIT)
* Writing & Reading the Essay (MIT)
* Academic Writing sequence (Coursera)
* Optional: Grammar & Style (edX)

~20 hrs/week total.

### **Phase 2 (Months 4–8) — Core Foundations**

* Masterpieces of World Literature (HarvardX)
* American Literature 1865–Present (MIT)
* Introduction to Linguistic Theory (MIT)
* Writing About Literature (MIT)
* History of the English Language (Textbook)

### **Phase 3 (Months 9–14) — Theory + Methods**

* MIT: Introduction to Literary Theory
* ModPo (UPenn)
* Research Methods (Open University)
* Advanced writing course of your choice

### **Phase 4 (Months 15–20) — Specialization Track**

Pick **one** of the 4 tracks, complete all courses.

### **Phase 5 (Months 21–24) — Capstone**

4–8 hours/week research + optional course pairing.

# **Final Project (Capstone)**

A substantial, polished project suitable for peer review or public sharing.

### **Goal**

Demonstrate mastery of literary/linguistic/rhetorical analysis through original research, argument, or digital project.

### **Possible Project Types**

* A 25–35 page research paper using close reading + theory
* A digital text-analysis project (for DH track)
* A rhetorical analysis portfolio + long-form argumentative essay
* A comparative literature study (2–3 primary texts)

### **Milestones**

1. **Proposal (Week 1–2)**

   * 2–3 pages: topic, question, primary texts, method, preliminary bibliography.
2. **Annotated Bibliography (Week 3–4)**

   * 12–20 scholarly sources with short annotations.
3. **Midterm Draft (Week 6–8)**

   * Full argument skeleton + 8–12 pages drafted.
4. **Peer Review + Revision (Week 9–10)**

   * Share via online communities (Reddit r/AskLiteraryStudies, Discord reading groups, Coursera forums).
5. **Final Deliverable (Week 12)**

   * 25–35 page research paper or digital project with a methodological appendix.

### **Evaluation Rubric**

* **Argument Quality (30%)** — Logical structure, coherence, justified claims.
* **Depth & Originality (30%)** — Interpretive insight; intellectual risk-taking.
* **Use of Evidence (20%)** — Textual support, theoretical integration, scholarly sources.
* **Clarity & Style (10%)** — Voice, grammar, precision.
* **Presentation & Scholarly Apparatus (10%)** — MLA formatting, citations, appendix.
